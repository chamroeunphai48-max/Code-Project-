Origins

Buddhism was founded over 2,500 years ago in ancient India by Siddhartha Gautama, who became known as the Buddha, meaning "the awakened one." Born a prince, he renounced his luxurious life to seek the truth about suffering and the path to liberation.

üåø

Philosophy

Buddhism is both a religion and a philosophy that offers a practical path to understanding the nature of reality, reducing suffering, and achieving inner peace. It emphasizes personal spiritual development and the attainment of deep insight into the true nature of life.

üåç

Global Reach

Today, Buddhism is practiced by over 500 million people worldwide. It has spread across Asia and beyond, adapting to different cultures while maintaining its core teachings of wisdom, compassion, and mindfulness.
