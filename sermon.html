Buddha's first sermon

the Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta ("Setting in Motion the Wheel of Dharma"), was delivered at Deer Park, Sarnath, India, to his first five disciples, introducing the core Buddhist teachings: the Four Noble Truths (life is suffering, its cause is craving, it can end, and the path is the Eightfold Path) and the Middle Way (avoiding extremes of indulgence and self-mortification).

Four Noble Truths

Dukkha (suffering)

It's the first of the Four Noble Truths, highlighting that life involves inevitable stress, disappointment, and the frustration of not getting what we want.

Samudaya (Origin)

In Buddhism, it's the Second Noble Truth, identifying the cause of suffering (dukkha) as craving, attachment, or desire (tanhā), which fuels the cycle of rebirth and dissatisfaction.

Nirodha (Cessation)

Nirodha (Sanskrit and Pali) fundamentally means cessation, restraint, or control. In a spiritual context, it is a key concept in both Buddhism and Hinduism (Yoga).
